## Section B: Short Answer (30 points)

**Question 11 (6 points)**
Explain the difference between a "Quick Win" and a "Strategic Pilot" on the use case prioritisation matrix. When would you pursue each?

**Sample Answer:**
A Quick Win is high impact, low effort - perfect for proving value quickly with minimal resources. A Strategic Pilot is high impact, high effort - larger projects that require more investment but offer significant returns. You'd pursue Quick Wins first to build momentum and prove value, then move to Strategic Pilots once you have buy-in and resources.

---

**Question 12 (6 points)**
A company wants to use an AI agent to screen job applications. What ethical considerations should they address before deployment?

**Sample Answer:**
Key considerations include: (1) Bias in training data that could discriminate against certain groups, (2) Transparency about how decisions are made, (3) Privacy of applicant data, (4) Human oversight of final decisions, (5) Appeal process for rejected candidates, (6) Regular audits for fairness.

---

**Question 13 (6 points)**
Describe how you would calculate ROI for an AI agent implementation. What metrics would you track?

**Sample Answer:**
ROI = (Benefits - Costs) / Costs × 100%. Benefits include: labour time saved, error reduction, revenue increase, customer satisfaction improvement. Costs include: AI/LLM API costs, development/integration, training, maintenance. Metrics: time saved per task, error rates before/after, customer satisfaction scores, adoption rate.

---

**Question 14 (6 points)**
What are the four phases of the AI Strategy Framework, and why is each important?

**Sample Answer:**
(1) Assess - Understand current state, capabilities, and gaps. (2) Align - Connect AI initiatives to business goals. (3) Plan - Develop roadmap, resources, timeline. (4) Execute - Implement, measure, iterate. Each phase is crucial because skipping one leads to misaligned or failed implementations.

---

**Question 15 (6 points)**
Explain the three options for Human-in-the-Loop and when each is most appropriate.

**Sample Answer:**
(1) Human reviews before action - for high-stakes decisions like hiring or medical. (2) Human monitors and can intervene - for ongoing processes where errors are costly but not critical. (3) Human reviews after action - for lower-risk tasks where speed matters more than perfection.

## Section C: Scenario Analysis (30 points)

**Question 16 (15 points)**
*Scenario:* A mid-sized accounting firm wants to implement AI agents to handle client inquiries, schedule appointments, and prepare basic financial reports.

**Tasks:**
a) Identify 3 potential high-impact use cases for AI agents in this firm (3 points)
b) What ethical concerns should they address? (4 points)
c) Outline a 90-day implementation roadmap (4 points)
d) What metrics would you track to measure success? (4 points)

**Sample Answer:**

a) Use Cases:
- Client inquiry handling (FAQ, status checks)
- Appointment scheduling and calendar management
- Basic report preparation and data compilation

b) Ethical Concerns:
- Client data privacy and confidentiality
- Accuracy of financial information
- Human oversight of sensitive decisions
- Transparency about AI use to clients
- Job displacement for junior staff

c) 90-Day Roadmap:
- Days 1-30: Assess current processes, select pilot use case, set up OpenClaw
- Days 31-60: Deploy first agent, test with small client group, gather feedback
- Days 61-90: Refine agent, expand to additional use cases, train staff

d) Success Metrics:
- Time saved per task
- Client satisfaction scores
- Error rates
- Number of inquiries handled
- Cost per interaction

---

**Question 17 (15 points)**
*Scenario:* A retail company plans to replace their customer service team with AI agents. The CEO says "AI is cheaper and can work 24/7."

**Tasks:**
a) What are the risks of this approach? (4 points)
b) What alternative approach would you recommend? (4 points)
c) How would you measure whether the AI implementation is successful? (4 points)
d) What ethical considerations apply? (3 points)

**Sample Answer:**

a) Risks:
- Customer frustration with AI limitations
- Loss of human empathy in sensitive situations
- Brand reputation damage from poor AI interactions
- Legal liability if AI gives wrong advice
- Employee morale and job losses

b) Alternative Approach:
- Hybrid model: AI handles routine inquiries, humans handle complex/sensitive issues
- AI assists humans rather than replacing them
- Gradual transition with human oversight

c) Success Metrics:
- Customer satisfaction (CSAT)
- First-contact resolution rate
- Average handling time
- Escalation rate to human agents
- Cost per interaction

d) Ethical Considerations:
- Transparency about AI use
- Job displacement and retraining
- Fair treatment of all customers
- Data privacy
- Accountability for AI errors
